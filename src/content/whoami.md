They call me Genix.

Backend engineer, open-source tinkerer, and a person who has had a computer as a constant companion since age four — first as a toy, then slowly as a window into how anything works at all.

I trace most of what I do back to one habit: asking "what is this, actually?" about things everyone treats as obvious. That habit turned a casual question about arrays into ARLIZ — a three-volume descent from a voltage difference across a transistor up to tensor cores. It also means I build systems that trade simplicity for scale, and keep questioning abstractions until they either hold up or fall apart in my hands.

Most of my work lives somewhere between distributed messaging, event-driven architectures, and the philosophy of data structures.

Still learning. Still breaking my own designs on purpose, just to see where they bend.
